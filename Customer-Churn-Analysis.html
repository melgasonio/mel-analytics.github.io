Customer Churn Analysis

The operations manager of ComConnect, Tricia, requested our team to make a new project. This time, she wants us to conduct a Customer Churn Analysis.

But first of all, let us define Customer Churn or Customer Attrition in other words. In the dynamic landscape of business, customer churn stands as a significant challenge for companies across industries. Customer churn, often referred to as customer attrition or turnover, is the phenomenon where customers cease their relationship with a business, either by switching to a competitor or by completely discontinuing the use of a service or product. It's a metric that directly impacts a company's revenue, growth, and overall success.

The following are the steps of making a Customer Churn Analysis on Powr BI.

Step 1: Data Collection and Preparation

  • Data Sources: Gather relevant data sources, including customer information, transaction history, and any other data that may be indicative of customer behavior.

  • Data Cleaning: Clean and preprocess the data to handle missing values, duplicates, and outliers. Ensure that the data is in a format suitable for analysis.

    • Step 2: Load Data into Power BI

  • Connect to Data Sources: Open Power BI and connect to your prepared data sources.

  • Data Modeling: Create relationships between different tables if necessary. Define measures and calculated columns that will be useful for churn analysis.

    • <

    Step 3: Define Churn

  • Churn Definition: Clearly define what constitutes churn for your analysis. It could be customers who have not made a purchase in a certain time frame or those who have canceled their subscriptions.

    • Step 4: Create Churn Metrics

  • Churn Rate Calculation: Use DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) in Power BI to calculate churn rates. This may involve creating a measure that divides the number of churned customers by the total number of customers over a specific period.

    • Step 5: Visualize Churn Trends

  • Create Visualizations: Use Power BI's visualization tools to create charts and graphs that show churn trends over time. Line charts, area charts, or tables can be effective for this purpose.

  • Segmentation: Explore segmenting the data to understand if certain customer groups have higher churn rates.
